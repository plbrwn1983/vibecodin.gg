# Domain Hubs & Sub-domains

## Overview

The platform is organized into twelve domain hubs covering business functions common across most standard business models. Each hub contains sub-domains that define where contributions belong. Further granularity beyond sub-domain is handled by tags — there are no sub-sub-domains.

### The Twelve Hubs
1. Marketing
2. Sales
3. Finance & Accounting
4. Human Resources
5. Operations
6. Customer Service
7. Legal & Compliance
8. IT & Technology
9. Procurement
10. Logistics
11. Executive & Strategy
12. Product Development

## Cross-Cutting Boundary Decisions

Where topics appear across multiple hubs, ownership is resolved as follows. Tags handle discoverability across hubs — contributions are not duplicated.

| Topic | Primary Hub | Boundary Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Contract management | Legal & Compliance | Legal owns drafting, review, and legal risk. Procurement owns contract lifecycle and compliance tracking for vendor agreements specifically. |
| Analytics & reporting | Each respective hub | Every hub owns its domain-specific analytics. Tags connect related analytics contributions across hubs. |
| Vendor/supplier management | Procurement | Procurement owns vendor selection, sourcing, and onboarding. Operations owns ongoing day-to-day coordination post-award. |
| Workplace safety | Operations | Operations owns day-to-day safety protocols and incident management. HR owns employee wellness, occupational health programs, and safety training design. |
| Data privacy | Legal & Compliance | Legal owns regulatory compliance obligations and frameworks. IT & Technology owns technical implementation of privacy controls. |
| Risk management | Executive & Strategy | Executive & Strategy owns enterprise-wide risk strategy. Operations owns operational continuity planning. Legal & Compliance owns regulatory and legal risk. |
| Technical support | Customer Service | Customer Service owns customer-facing technical troubleshooting. IT & Technology owns internal IT help desk and systems support. |
| Returns processing | Customer Service | Customer Service owns the customer-facing refund and complaint resolution process. Logistics owns physical reverse logistics and restocking operations. |
| Performance management | Human Resources | HR owns employee performance reviews and goal-setting. Executive & Strategy owns organizational and business performance measurement. |
| Payroll | Finance & Accounting | Finance owns payroll processing and tax withholding. HR owns compensation strategy and benefits design. |
| Product QA | Product Development | Product Development owns QA for product features and user experience. Operations owns quality assurance for operational processes and service delivery. |
| Financial planning | Finance & Accounting | Finance owns budgeting and forecasting at the operational level. Executive & Strategy owns long-range financial planning and enterprise modeling. |
